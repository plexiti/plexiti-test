Domain-Driven Design? The Blue Book!

With „DDD“ (Domain-Driven Design) towards a shared and „ubiquituous“ language for business and software experts.

It was in 2003 already, when Eric Evans wrote an impressive book: „Domain-Driven Design – Tackling Complexity in the Heart of Software“. More than a decade later, I believe one can justifiably conclude that „DDD“ has been a few steps ahead of its time. Evans wanted an alignment of business and software experts, which - in the context of contemporary agile organisations and distributed systems - appears to me more modern than ever. I therefore wrote an Introduction to Domain-Driven Design for the German magazine OBJEKTspektrum.

The blue book and the more recent red book

While some claim, that the book with its 563 pages partly reads somewhat „philosophical“, real DDD Trekkies call it just their „blue book“. Probably this is also because Vaughn Vernon wrote a few years ago a well-received „red book“ about „Implementing Domain-Driven Design“. I re-read both of them recently and can personally also strongly recommend both of them. They were written in different times and differ in personal writing styles. Together they cover the topic from the more design oriented fundamentals right through to real-life implementation with examples in plain old Java.

My own ultra short path for german readers

In the last edition of the well-read German software magazine OBJEKTspektrum, I wrote an Introduction to Domain-Driven Design, mainly for newcomers, guiding the reader through the more strategic and more tactical building blocks by means of a case in the domain of insurances. Here is the link to the PDF Domain-Driven Design? Na klar, das blaue Buch! - kindly made available to the general public by the publisher SIGS DATACOM. Thank you!

Vaughn Vernon distilled DDD - in english

In case you still want to know more, but don’t find the time to read more than 500 pages, I recommend to get started with Vaughn Vernon’s most recent book Domain-Driven Design Distilled. It’s a 176 pages paperback, really concise, readable and focuses on what you need to know to get first results. Later on you can still return to Eric Evan’s oeuvre and dig into the details of his thinking process. If you know the problem of tackling domain complexity and crunching a domain’s subtle knowledge in a communicable and then executable and maintainable form, I bet you will!
