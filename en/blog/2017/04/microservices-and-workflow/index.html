Microservices and Workflow

At the last meetup of the Camunda Vienna community, I gave a talk about the relationship of microservices with workflow.

The relationship of Microservices and Workflow is for sure not an easy one. While some members of the microservices community react with reservations or just dart off when the topic of workflow is brought up, members of the workflow community are either ignorant of microservices or rather curious about them, but then tend to act somewhat… awkward.

Claims and Sins of the Past

Furthermore, many too bold claims and sins of workflow in the past continue to make a revived relationship with new paradigms like Microservices difficult. An overemphasis on hierarchy and command-and-control-attitude by a certain species of dog lovers does not help either. Cat owners like to respect the autonomy of their pets and understand that the species is only driven by the events around them. Cats just make their very own decisions about what they are interested in - or not. You better understand that to get along with them!

The Future of Workflow?

Future-proof workflow needs to respect the fundamental ideas of decentral governance and therefore needs to be ready to work in a highly embedded and composable manner. Workflow tooling needs to be deployable as self-contained (micro-) services itself and work in a highly event-driven fashion. For a really bright future it would also need to respect the autonomous choices of other teams who decide to implement their process related requirements by other means. Clever workflow tooling would not just tolerate other systems, but integrate their “foreign” events into an end-to-end business process monitoring. Slide 47 shows a quite simple suggestion which does not yet exist, but is in reach on the basis of existing tools.
